Setting Tent

STEP 1: Lay the Base

Gather your tent, poles, rainfly and footprint or tarp.Choose a spot to set up your tent — as clear, level and flat as possible.Remove any sticks, pine cones, stones or debris on the ground where you will set up your tent.Lay down the footprint or tarp.

STEP 2: Spread Out and Stake Your Tent

Spread the tent across the tarp or footprint with a partner. Pull it tight and stake two opposite corners to firm up the floor. Drive stakes straight into the ground, hook facing out. Use a mallet, boot sole, or a log if needed. Stake the remaining corners. Pro Tip: Carry extra stakes for backups.

STEP 3: Add the Poles

Unfold the pole segments connected by bungee cords and click them together. Insert the main poles into the tent's exterior sleeves or clips, typically crossing at the tent’s peak. Slide the poles carefully to avoid snags. Attach any additional poles as needed.

STEP 4: Raise the Tent

Slowly raise one main pole, securing each end into the fasteners or pockets near the ground. Repeat with the cross pole and any additional support poles until the tent is fully upright. Attach any fasteners or clips along the poles to secure the tent.

STEP 5: Add the Rainfly

Drape the rainfly over the top of your tent. Insert any required poles for the rainfly, if applicable. Attach the rainfly to fasteners on the tent, typically located along or at the base of the support poles. Ensure it’s secure to effectively divert rainwater and keep the tent dry.

STEP 6: Add Final Stakes and Supports

Stake any remaining edges of the tent and secure ropes to keep it taut. Position and stake the rainfly, ensuring it doesn't obstruct campsite pathways to avoid trips and falls.
